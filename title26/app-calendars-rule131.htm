Rule 131. Report Calendars

On a calendar specifically set for the purpose or on a trial calendar, and after notice to the parties of the time and place, any case at issue may be listed and called, first, for report as to whether the case is to be tried or otherwise disposed of, and if the latter, for report as to its status; and, secondly, if it is to be tried, for report on the status of preparations for trial, with particular reference to the stipulation requirements of Rule 91. With respect to any case on such a calendar, the Court may consider other matters and take such action as it deems appropriate.
